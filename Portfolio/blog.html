Mahatma Gandhi

Gandhi, often referred to as Mahatma Gandhi or simply "Bapu," was an Indian lawyer, anti-colonial nationalist, and political ethicist who employed nonviolent resistance to lead the successful struggle for India's independence from British rule, and in turn inspired movements for civil rights and freedom across the world. Gandhi's works, teachings, and words have inspired freedom fighters and civil rights activists around the globe.

Gandhi was born on 2 October 1869 as Mohandas Karamchand Gandhi in Porbandar, a coastal town in Kathiawar (now Gujarat) in India. He was from a religious Hindu family and his father, Karamchand Gandhi, was a civil servant. He was an average student and later studied law in London.

Gautam Adani

Gautam Adani is an Indian businessman who is the founder and chairman of the Adani Group, a multinational conglomerate active in the energy, logistics, agribusiness, and defense sectors. With a net worth of nearly $32 billion, Adani is the richest person in India.

He began his business career in 1988 and has since made investments in multiple sectors, such as energy, logistics, and natural resources. He is known for his ambitious projects, such as the Mundra Port and the Carmichael coal mine project.

Adani is also active in philanthropy, contributing to various public health, education, and environmental initiatives.
